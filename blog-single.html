Training Tips

5 Drills for Better Timing

May 12, 2025 Coach James 4 min read

The catch is often considered the most critical part of the rowing stroke. It's the moment of connection, where the blade enters the water and the power phase begins. Get it right, and the boat glides effortlessly. Get it wrong, and you're fighting the water, bleeding speed, and risking injury.

1. The Placement Drill

Start from the finish position, square the blade, and slowly roll forward on the slide. As you approach the front stops, smoothly lift the hands to let the blade drop into the water before you apply pressure with the legs. The goal is to hear the "plop" of the blade dropping in without any splash toward the bow.

"The catch is not a hit; it's a placement. Think of dropping a coin into a slot."

2. Roll-Ups

Sit at the finish. Keep the blade squared. Row using only the top quarter of the slide. Focus intensely on the hand height. Are you dropping your hands too early? This drill exposes hand-height inconsistencies immediately.

3. Pause at the Release

This might seem counter-intuitive, but a good catch starts at the finish. By pausing at the release (arms away, body over), you ensure your body is prepped and your weight is distributed correctly before you even start sliding forward.

Conclusion

Perfecting the catch is a lifelong journey for any rower. Incorporate these drills into your daily warm-up, and over time, you'll feel the connection become sharper, quicker, and infinitely more powerful. See you on the water!
